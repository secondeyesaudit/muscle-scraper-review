What is muscle scraping?

Pain that other therapy
couldn't shift.

Muscle scraping sits under IASTM (Instrument Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilisation) — a myofascial release technique that relieves pain in the fascia, the connective tissue supporting your muscles, joints and bones.

Fascia should be elastic and movable. When it becomes thick, sticky or dried out it restricts movement and refers pain elsewhere — it's not unusual for shoulder pain to actually radiate from a tight calf. Compensating for the loss of movement quietly creates more tightness, leading to widespread discomfort.

Unlike a relaxing sports or deep-tissue massage, this is intense and at times painful. We use 304 surgical-grade steel bars, purpose-made to stretch tissue and break down knots that hands alone can't reach.

Does it hurt? Let's be honest

What to expect

It can be intense and, in places, painful — but it shouldn't be unbearable. Your therapist works to your limit and adjusts as they go, so tell them what you're feeling. Afterwards you may be sore, bruise, or feel a mild sunburn-like sensation for a day or two.

The pay-off is what keeps people coming back: greater range of motion, less pain, and less stiffness — often felt the very next day.

Nervous? You're in good company. First-timers regularly say "not as painful as I expected" and "I could have fallen asleep." Ask about starting with Kirsty for a gentler first session.

Cancellation policy

A minimum of 48 hours' notice is required to cancel. If you can't attend, either cancel at least 48 hours before, or arrange for someone to take your place. Less than 48 hours' notice (and no replacement) means the full session fee is charged — this lets us offer your slot to someone waiting.
